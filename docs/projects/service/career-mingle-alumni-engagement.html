Published

January 1, 2025

The Career Mingle and Alumni Engagement work reflects my service connecting students with alumni, employers, graduate programs, professional mentors, and career-development opportunities.

This work includes organizing the annual Geography and Sustainability Career Mingle, maintaining relationships with alumni, supporting the department alumni network, recruiting professionals to meet with students, and helping students identify internships, jobs, graduate programs, and professional pathways.

Service Context

Students in Geography, Sustainability, GIST, Urban Planning, and related environmental fields benefit from opportunities to talk directly with professionals. Many students know they are interested in maps, places, sustainability, environmental work, GIS, planning, or community problem-solving, but they may not yet know what those interests look like as careers.

The Career Mingle and related alumni-engagement work are designed to help students build those connections. The goal is to make career exploration more personal, more accessible, and more connected to real people doing relevant work.

Geography and Sustainability Career Mingle

In partnership with the UTK Center for Career Exploration and Development, I organize, recruit for, and host the annual Geography and Sustainability Career Mingle.

The event is designed as a reverse career fair. Instead of asking students to walk up to employer tables, students meet with professionals in short one-on-one conversations. The focus is on career exploration, professional storytelling, networking, and helping students understand possible pathways after graduation.

The sixth annual Career Mingle included:

  • 111 one-on-one sessions,
  • 50 undergraduate students,
  • 6 graduate students,
  • students from 20 different majors,
  • 19 employers,
  • 2 graduate schools,
  • recruiting visits to more than 20 upper-division classes.

Alumni Engagement

Alumni engagement is a major part of this work. I maintain relationships with alumni to support student opportunities, professional networking, internships, research connections, and post-graduation pathways.

This work includes:

  • managing the department alumni group on LinkedIn,
  • organizing informal alumni meet-ups in Knoxville,
  • helping maintain alumni relationships that support internships and job opportunities,
  • contributing to the department Alumni Award Ceremony,
  • nominating alumni for recognition,
  • supporting monthly Featured Alumni updates on the department website,
  • inviting alumni and professionals to participate in department events.

Student Career Support

Beyond the Career Mingle, I regularly share job, internship, fellowship, research, conference, and professional-development opportunities with students. I also support students through advising conversations, reference letters, informal mentoring, and connections to alumni and professional partners.

Recent student-support work has included:

  • writing reference letters for students and alumni,
  • sharing job and internship opportunities through student communication channels,
  • connecting students with professional organizations and conferences,
  • recruiting students for research and applied GIS opportunities,
  • coordinating with campus career-development staff,
  • helping students understand how their GIS, geography, sustainability, and planning skills connect to career pathways.

Partnership Building

This service work depends on sustained partnerships with alumni, employers, graduate programs, campus career-development staff, faculty, staff, and students.

A major part of the work is identifying professionals who are willing to share their experiences with students, explaining the value of the event, recruiting student participants, and making sure the event is useful for both students and professionals.

The work also supports the department by strengthening relationships with alumni and external partners who can provide internships, research opportunities, guest speakers, field trips, project partnerships, and employment pathways.

Service Significance

The Career Mingle and alumni-engagement work reflect my commitment to helping students see where their education can take them. Career development is not something that happens only at the end of a degree. Students benefit from repeated opportunities to meet professionals, ask questions, hear career stories, and imagine possible futures.

This work also strengthens the department’s professional network. By connecting students, alumni, employers, graduate programs, and campus career-development partners, the Career Mingle helps build a stronger community around Geography, Sustainability, GIST, and related fields.
