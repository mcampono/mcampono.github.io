Department service supporting events, communications, student visibility, geospatial infrastructure, and the day-to-day systems that help the department function.
Published

January 1, 2024

The Department Events, Communications, and Geospatial Infrastructure Support page highlights a broad set of department service work that supports visibility, community-building, student opportunities, events, communications, and geospatial teaching infrastructure.

Much of this work happens behind the scenes, but it is essential to the day-to-day function of the Department of Geography and Sustainability. It helps students present their work, faculty and staff communicate with audiences, events run smoothly, and geospatial teaching spaces remain useful for classes, outreach, and demonstrations.

Service Context

Departments depend on more than formal courses and research projects. They also need events, communications systems, shared equipment, student support, professional visibility, and spaces where faculty, staff, students, alumni, and visitors can connect.

My department service in this area focuses on maintaining and improving those systems. This includes supporting department events, digital and physical communications, geospatial equipment, student poster printing, teaching spaces, and public-facing department visibility.

Department Events

I help organize, support, and promote several department events each year. These events create opportunities for students, faculty, staff, alumni, and community partners to gather, celebrate accomplishments, and connect with one another.

Recent event-support work includes:

  • organizing and contributing to the department end-of-year celebration,
  • helping organize the Alumni Award Ceremony,
  • recruiting and organizing Lunch and Learn speakers,
  • supporting Geography Awareness Week speaker recruitment and organizing,
  • creating event signs and promotional materials,
  • hosting guest speakers and department visitors,
  • supporting informal out-of-class community-building events.

Communications and Visibility

I support department communications through a mix of digital, print, and in-building materials. This work helps keep students informed, highlights faculty and staff, promotes department events, and makes department spaces more welcoming and useful.

This work includes:

  • maintaining digital signage,
  • updating the department website,
  • adding events to the campus event calendar,
  • updating Featured Alumni content,
  • creating and designing faculty and staff posters,
  • updating staff directories on each floor of Burchfiel Geography Building,
  • updating faculty bulletin boards,
  • creating signs and graphics for department events,
  • managing the department alumni LinkedIn group.

Student Poster and Presentation Support

A major part of my department service is helping students prepare professional-quality posters and visual materials for classes, conferences, research presentations, and outreach events.

This includes maintaining the department plotter, managing printing supplies, troubleshooting print issues, and printing student posters for events such as:

  • SEDAAG,
  • AAG,
  • EUReCA,
  • AURA,
  • TNGIC,
  • class projects,
  • student research presentations.

This support helps students present their work professionally and lowers barriers for students participating in academic and professional conferences.

Geospatial Infrastructure Support

I help maintain department geospatial teaching and demonstration infrastructure, including equipment used in classes, labs, outreach, and public demonstrations.

This work includes support for:

  • the department plotter,
  • the SimTable,
  • the Augmented Reality Sandbox,
  • the Mixed Reality Lab,
  • GIS lab hardware and software,
  • teaching equipment used for hands-on class activities,
  • lab inventory and updates,
  • software updates and testing.

The SimTable and Augmented Reality Sandbox are used in several hands-on lab activities across the department, helping students and visitors explore terrain, hazards, watersheds, and other geographic processes in interactive ways.

GIS Lab and Technical Support

I also support the GIS Lab Manager and department geospatial technology needs. This work includes helping develop funding requests, supporting Tech Fee and end-of-fiscal-year equipment planning, assisting with lab hardware and software updates, and helping maintain inventories.

Recent support work has included:

  • helping prepare funding requests,
  • supporting lab computer hardware and software updates,
  • assisting with equipment inventory,
  • helping rebuild and reorganize spaces after flooding,
  • updating software,
  • rewiring hardware,
  • reassembling desks and tables,
  • inventorying labs, conference rooms, and storage spaces.

Service Significance

This service work reflects my commitment to making the department function well for students, faculty, staff, alumni, and visitors. Some of this work is highly visible, such as events and digital signage. Other parts are less visible, such as maintaining equipment, printing posters, updating software, managing supplies, and troubleshooting technology.

Together, these contributions support the department’s teaching, outreach, research, recruitment, and community-building missions. They help students share their work, help faculty and staff communicate with audiences, and help maintain the geospatial infrastructure needed for hands-on learning.
