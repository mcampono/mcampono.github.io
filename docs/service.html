Service & Leadership

Service has been a central part of my professional career. I believe that geospatial education is strengthened through collaboration, professional engagement, and community involvement. My service activities focus on expanding opportunities for students, supporting the geospatial profession, strengthening educational programs, and connecting universities with practitioners, educators, and community partners.

These leadership roles have included departmental service, statewide professional organizations, conference planning, outreach initiatives, strategic planning, and collaborative projects that extend beyond the classroom.

Areas of Service

  • Professional Organizations: Leadership and committee service supporting the geospatial profession and geospatial education.
  • Conference Planning: Organizing conferences, workshops, and educational programming that connect students, educators, professionals, and community partners.
  • Program Development: Creating new educational opportunities, outreach programs, workshops, and student experiences.
  • Department and University Service: Supporting curriculum development, student engagement, outreach, recruitment, and program growth.
  • Community Engagement: Building partnerships that connect university resources with public agencies, nonprofit organizations, educators, and local communities.

Philosophy of Service

I view service as an extension of teaching. Bringing people together, creating opportunities, sharing resources, and helping organizations solve problems all contribute to strengthening the geospatial community.

Many of the most rewarding projects I have worked on have resulted from long-term relationships between universities, professional organizations, educators, students, and community partners.
