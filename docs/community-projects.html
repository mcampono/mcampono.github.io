Community GIS Projects

My community-engaged GIS work focuses on using geospatial tools to support partner organizations, public-facing projects, applied research, and student learning. These projects often combine mapping, spatial analysis, UAV data collection, remote sensing, cartography, and communication with real-world questions and community needs.

Many of these projects also serve as learning opportunities for students. They provide a way to connect technical GIS skills with professional expectations, partner communication, project management, data limitations, and public-facing deliverables.

Project Areas

  • Historic Preservation and Cultural Landscapes: Mapping and documenting cemeteries, historic sites, and community landscapes.
  • Environmental and Conservation Mapping: Supporting conservation, land management, trail planning, and natural-resource questions.
  • Public Infrastructure and Facilities: Using GIS and UAV data to support inspection, documentation, planning, and communication.
  • Community Partner Support: Developing maps, analyses, and geospatial products for organizations with specific applied needs.
  • Student-Engaged Applied GIS: Creating opportunities for students to participate in meaningful geospatial projects beyond the classroom.

Approach

Community GIS projects require more than technical analysis. A successful project depends on understanding the partner’s question, identifying appropriate data, communicating limitations, and producing deliverables that are useful to the intended audience.

I try to approach these projects as collaborative problem-solving efforts. The goal is not simply to create a map or dataset, but to help partners better understand places, patterns, conditions, and decisions.
